# GOVERNANCE.md: Dispatcher AO Joint Operating Node

## 1. Objective
This document establishes the binding operational framework for the Dispatcher AO engine. This entity operates as a **Joint Operating Node** between **Nascent Labs & Technologies (NL&T)** and **Supreme Computation (SC)**.

## 2. Governance Structure
* **Governance Principle:** 66% Supermajority voting required for architectural shifts, capital deployment, or structural changes.
* **CEO (Nascent Labs Node):** Final authority on Systemic Laws, FWMP compliance, and patent strategy.
* **CTO (Supreme Computation Node):** Final authority on Main-Trunk Infrastructure, CI/CD Coherence, and Node Engineering.

## 3. Intellectual Property (IP) & Asset Pool
* **Joint IP Pool:** All code, protocols, and documentation within the `Dispatcher AO` repository are held in shared ownership.
* **License:** Both founding nodes maintain a perpetual, royalty-free license to utilize the engine for independent business operations.

## 4. CTO Equity & Vesting (The Coherence Gates)
The CTO node’s equity stake vests according to **System Coherence Milestones**:
* **Milestone I (Cliff):** Successful deployment of the Nine-Gate SCQOS validation harness in production.
* **Milestone II:** First successful live integration with a field-service node.
* **Milestone III:** Sustained FWMP packet throughput at defined metrics.

## 5. Strategic Node Integration (The "On-Deck" Provision)
To maintain structural integrity during the development phase:
* **Restricted Access:** Future nodes (including external manufacturing or logistics partners) shall be restricted from formal JV equity participation until the engine achieves "Production Coherence" (defined as the successful completion of Milestone I).
* **Integration Trigger:** Future node integration requires a 66% supermajority agreement and shall be carved out of the joint pool in a manner that preserves the foundational partnership proportions.

## 6. Dissolution & IP Reversion
Should the partnership cease to function, the core IP shall revert to an open-source model under the MIT License, ensuring the continued viability of the Dispatcher AO technology.
